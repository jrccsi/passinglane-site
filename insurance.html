Teen Auto Insurance

The moment your teen starts supervised practice, your insurance needs to know. Adding a teen driver increases rates — but not disclosing them can void your coverage in an accident. Here's what to do and when.

When to contact your insurer

  • Before supervised practice begins — report your teen as an occasional driver once they have a permit
  • Again when they're licensed — add them as a named driver once the provisional license is issued
  • Rates will increase — now is a good time to compare across providers
  • Teen drivers typically add $1,000–$3,000/year to a policy depending on vehicle and location
  • Good student discounts (B average or better) can reduce the increase by 10–25%
Important for parents: Most policies require you to disclose household members operating your vehicle. Failing to add your teen before an accident could result in a denied claim or policy cancellation.

🚦 Defensive Driving & Traffic School

For California teen drivers, a defensive driving or court-approved traffic school course can be a valuable step after a traffic violation. It reinforces safe driving habits, helps teens understand the consequences of risky driving, and may help prevent future citations or collisions.

  • Traffic Violator School — eligible violations may allow the driver to attend so the conviction is kept confidential and does not add a negligent-operator point to the public driving record. Eligibility is determined by the court and DMV rules.
  • Insurance considerations — completing a course may result in possible discounts or reduced premium impact, but that decision is made by the insurance carrier and should be confirmed before enrolling.
